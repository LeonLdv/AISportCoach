# NTRP Rating Guide — What Is Your NTRP Rating?
---

## Overview

The NTRP (National Tennis Rating Program) scale runs from **1.0 to 7.0**.
Use this guide to assess a player's skill level from video or self-evaluation.

---

## Quick Reference — Simplified Tiers

| Tier | NTRP Range | Summary |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Beginner | 1.0 – 2.5 | Slow-paced rallies; limited experience; struggles to find contact point; focus is stroke development |
| Intermediate | 2.5 – 4.0 | Baseline rallies; recognises finishing opportunities; consistent on medium pace; lacks depth, direction, court awareness |
| Advanced | 4.5 – 5.5 | Varies pace and spin; good court coverage and depth control; can build game plans; challenges are more mental than technical |
| World Class | 6.0 – 7.0 | Professional / elite level |

## Detailed Breakdown by Stroke

### Beginner Range — NTRP 1.0 to 3.0

| Stroke | NTRP 1.0–1.5 | NTRP 2.0 | NTRP 2.5 | NTRP 3.0 |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Forehand** | Just starting; working on getting ball in play | Incomplete swing; no directional intent | Form developing; prepared for moderate pace | Fairly consistent; some directional intent; lacks depth control |
| **Backhand** | Just starting; working on getting ball in play | Avoids backhand; erratic contact; grip problems; incomplete swing | Preparation problems; often chooses forehand instead | Frequently prepared; fair consistency on moderate shots |
| **Serve & Return** | Just starting; working on getting ball in play | Incomplete motion; frequent double faults; inconsistent toss; erratic return | Full swing attempt; can get ball in play slowly; returns slow serves | Developing rhythm; little consistency with power; second serve much slower; fair return consistency |
| **Volley** | Just starting; working on getting ball in play | Reluctant at net; avoids backhand; lacks footwork | Uncomfortable at net, especially backhand; uses forehand face on backhand volleys | Consistent FH volley; inconsistent BH volley; trouble with low/wide shots |

### Intermediate–Advanced Range — NTRP 3.5 to 5.0

| Stroke | NTRP 3.5 | NTRP 4.0 | NTRP 4.5 | NTRP 5.0 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Forehand** | Improved consistency and variety; directional control; developing spin | Good consistency; depth and control on moderate shots; may over-hit on difficult shots | Very good consistency; effective pace and spin; controls depth; offensive on moderate shots | Strong with control, depth, spin; sets up offensive situations; consistent passing shots |
| **Backhand** | Directional control on moderate shots; difficulty on high/hard shots; defensive on difficult shots | Consistent depth and direction on moderate shots; developing spin | Controls direction and depth; may break down under pressure; offensive on moderate shots | Aggressive and consistent; good direction and depth on most shots; varies spin |
| **Serve & Return** | Serve: control with some power; developing spin. Return: consistent with directional control on moderate shots | Places first and second serves; uses spin on first; dependable return; depth in singles; mixes returns in doubles | Aggressive serving; limited double faults; power and spin; good depth on second serve; frequently hits aggressive returns; some success taking pace off in doubles | Serve placed to exploit weakness or create offense; variety of serves; good depth/spin/placement on second serve to force weak return; mixes aggressive and off-pace returns with control |
| **Volley** | Covers side shots; proper footwork; directs FH volleys; controls BH volley but little offense; difficulty putting away | Depth and control on FH volley; can direct BH volleys but lacks depth; developing wide/low volleys | Handles mixed volley sequences; good footwork; depth and directional control on BH; developing touch; tendency to overhit | Most volleys hit with depth, pace, and direction; handles difficult volleys with depth; often hits opportunity volleys for winners |

## Level Profiles — LLM Quick-Scan Format

### NTRP 1.0–1.5 — Absolute Beginner
- Just starting tennis
- Primary focus: getting the ball in play
- No consistent stroke mechanics yet

### NTRP 2.0 — Early Beginner
- Incomplete swing mechanics across all strokes
- Avoids backhand; grip and contact problems
- Frequent double faults; erratic returns
- Reluctant to approach net

### NTRP 2.5 — Developing Beginner
- Stroke form is developing; handles moderate pace
- Preparation issues; tends to favour forehand
- Attempts full service swing; can serve at slow pace
- Uncomfortable at net, especially on backhand side

### NTRP 3.0 — High Beginner / Low Intermediate
- Fairly consistent on moderate shots
- Some directional intent but lacks depth control
- Developing serve rhythm; second serve significantly slower than first
- Consistent forehand volley; backhand volley inconsistent

### NTRP 3.5 — Intermediate
- Improved consistency and variety; controls direction
- Developing spin on forehand and serve
- Defensive on high or hard backhand shots
- Covers side shots at net with proper footwork

### NTRP 4.0 — Solid Intermediate
- Good consistency with depth and control on moderate shots
- Directs backhand with consistency; developing spin
- Places first and second serves; uses spin; reliable return
- Depth and control on forehand volley; backhand volley lacks depth

### NTRP 4.5 — Advanced Intermediate
- Very good consistency; effective use of pace and spin
- Controls depth; offensive on moderate-paced shots
- Aggressive serving with few double faults; attacks second serves
- Handles mixed volley sequences; good backhand volley depth

### NTRP 5.0 — Advanced
- Strong groundstrokes with control, depth, and spin
- Uses forehand and backhand to create offensive situations
- Serve placed strategically to exploit weaknesses; varied repertoire
- Hits most volleys for depth, pace, and direction; finishes with winners

### NTRP 5.5–6.0 — Highly Advanced / Semi-Professional
- Near-complete game; challenges are primarily mental
- Consistent under pressure; executes tactics reliably

### NTRP 6.0–7.0 — World Class / Professional
- Elite competitive level

## Key Distinguishing Signals (for Video Analysis)

| Observation | Likely NTRP |
|-------------|------------|
| Can't make consistent contact | 1.0–1.5 |
| Avoids backhand; double faults often | 2.0 |
| Handles slow pace; struggles with net | 2.5 |
| Baseline rallies; no depth control | 3.0 |
| Directional control; developing spin | 3.5 |
| Consistent depth; places serve | 4.0 |
| Aggressive serve; offensive groundstrokes | 4.5 |
| Spin variation; volleys for winners | 5.0 |
| Tactical, high-pressure consistency | 5.5+ |
